Coraline by Neil Gaiman

Coraline by Neil Gaiman, with eerie illustrations by Dave McKean, is a dark fantasy about a girl who discovers a parallel world behind a hidden door. At first it seems better, but her “other” parents’ button-eyed smiles conceal a sinister truth. It’s a chilling tale of courage, curiosity, and confronting fear.

The Metamorphosis by Franz Kafka

Franz Kafka’s The Metamorphosis is a surreal novella about Gregor Samsa, a salesman who wakes up as a giant insect. As his family rejects him, the story explores themes of alienation, identity, isolation, and the fragility of human relationships and existence.

The Necronomicon by H.P. Lovecraft

The Necronomicon is a fictional grimoire in H.P. Lovecraft’s cosmic horror mythos. Written by the “mad Arab” Abdul Alhazred, it holds forbidden knowledge and dark rituals. It symbolizes the danger of seeking truths beyond human comprehension.

Ritual in the Dark by Colin Wilson

Ritual in the Dark by Colin Wilson is a psychological thriller set in post-war Europe. It follows Stephen, a young man drawn into violence and dark rituals while grappling with alienation and existential questions.

1984 by George Orwell

1984 by George Orwell is a dystopian novel about a society under constant surveillance. The story follows Winston Smith, who struggles against a totalitarian regime. It’s a powerful warning about censorship, control, and lost freedom.

The Crucible by Arthur Miller

The Crucible is a dramatic retelling of the Salem witch trials of 1692. Arthur Miller uses the hysteria to explore themes of fear, accusation, and integrity. Written during McCarthyism, it’s a powerful allegory about mass paranoia and injustice.
